100 Business Analysis Wisdoms
I Want My Kid To Know

Unlocking Life’s Wisdom for Both Kids and Adults

Have you ever wondered how early we can begin teaching kids valuable life lessons? This heartwarming and insightful book takes you on a 100-day journey where a mother shares practical advice, usually reserved for business and management, with her 9-year-old daughter. Through relatable, everyday examples, these complex concepts come to life in a fun and simple way that children can easily understand. From handling frustration and embracing creativity to managing time and focusing on what truly matters, each day brings a new piece of wisdom — designed not only for kids but with adults in mind too. What began as a challenge to teach valuable lessons to a child turned into a reflection for the author herself, revealing that these timeless truths are relevant at any age. This book is a collection of those 100 lessons, simplified and made engaging for children, but just as meaningful for parents, teachers, and anyone eager to learn and grow. Whether you're looking to share life advice with your kids or find reminders for your own journey, this book offers ideas and inspiration for everyone.

Business Analysis Courses for Real Results

Do you sometimes feel that, despite having perfectly crafted requirements, nothing moves forward? Or that after a seemingly successful meeting, progress just stalls? The truth is, true success in business analysis isn’t only about having great documentation. It’s about mastering communication, collaboration, and organization—along with a healthy dose of common sense. In these courses, inspired by 100 Business Analysis Wisdoms, you’ll dive deeper than the book itself, with practical, example-based lessons. These are the real-world insights you won’t find in traditional courses or management books—presented in a simple, straightforward way, making them easy to apply in your day-to-day work. Here are just a few things you’ll learn:
  1. How to influence stakeholders when logical arguments aren’t enough.
  2. Getting teams to act, even when agreements seem to lose momentum.
  3. Breaking down complexity using practical examples.
  4. Turning everyday challenges into opportunities for real progress.
And that’s just the beginning. These aren’t just business analysis courses—they’re your guide to unlocking practical wisdom that will help you turn ideas into results, and meetings into meaningful action.
